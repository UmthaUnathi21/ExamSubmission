Blog Post 4 - Close Reading - Data-Driven Artwork:

Goal & Aims:

I wanted to digitize an international basketball moment that is very special to me and basketball fanatics alike.

I wanted to initially represent the Springboks winning the World Cup however due to the constraints of the data processor I have in place the image was not viable because I require black and white images or colour images with a lot of contrast and no picture I found quite matched the description and after multiple tries reverted to basketball.

My aim was to find an iconic picture that eloquently displayed the historic 2016-2017 season of the Golden State Warriors (1946-present). A team that won 73 of 82 in-season games, a win percentage of 89%. This picture is one that reflects their confidence and mentality of being widely considered the greatest NBA (National Basketball Association, 1964- present) team ever.

Process:

I first found my desired picture (displayed below) and edited it on my phones built-in editor. I played around with aspects such as;

Exposure – toggling the exposure affects the amount of light that is displayed, creating overexposure visual data leads to pronounced highlights and fading effects in images

Brilliance – Increasing this setting allows for brighter image display specifically where the subject is concerned,

Highlights – I was able to increase the focus on the lightest parts of the image which pronounced the darker spots,

Shadows – To be able to make sure the processor was able to pick the light/highlights of the subjects in the picture I increased the dark tones of the shadows thus aiding in contrast of the people in the picture,

Contrast – I needed to create a high-contrast image in order for the highlights/lighter parts to be picked up and for the shadows to be pure black when rendered to create an outline of the main subject,

Brightness – The image itself needed to have a higher overall brightness than darkness thus increasing the brightness achieved that,

Black Point – Increasing the black point meant to increase the darkness in the darkest parts of the image where the shadows are this helped to create contrast in the image as a whole and allow for the rendered picture to be very prominent/detailed,

Vibrancy – This setting aided me in increasing the lighter shadows that were causing the image to be unclear when rendered,

Definition – Ultimately this setting makes sure that every detail that needs to displayed is micro-contrasted allowing each detailed to be seen when it otherwise would not have been.

I think this image is effective as a data visualization artwork because it’s personal to me and has a story attached to it thus increasing audience engagement and ultimately the understanding of the artwork “the use of narratives about the data can benefit the analysts by appealing to the emotions of the reader, but it also benefits the reader by revealing more context.” (Lund, 2022).

The use of the Golden State Warriors record-breaking NBA season record of ‘73-9’ as a means of representing the image in question I believe creates an even deeper narrative within the narrative of data visualization and speaks to the idea of “the effectiveness of the visualization in communicating to the audience” (Camm, Fry & Shaffer, 2017).

Effectiveness:

I think the collaboration of both the representation of Stephen Curry, Star-player of the Golden State Warriors NBA basketball team, and the teams most successful in-season campaign record ‘73-9’ reveals to the audience what is being represented and the significance of it in not only the history of the NBA (National Basketball Association, 1964- present) but also the history of sports.

This data-driven artwork truly functions as a piece of data-art by forming part of the four “typologies” (Lund, 2022) by revealing the significance of the data and entertaining viewers/audiences (Lund, 2022).

Furthermore, as a digital arts student myself I am made to understand artworks of any type to be meaningful to the artist and the audience, thus the effectiveness of an artwork is dependent upon the artists ability to relate their means of representation with the cultural norms/practices in society. I view this artwork as an effective means of communication and representation by using multiple data sources to create the artwork.

As a digital artist, in creating this artwork I have created a new of thinking about data because I now understand that a vast range of data such as image data and statistics, such as the Golden State Warriors NBA record, can be used to create digital artworks. This can be achieved through a variety of digital mediums; HTML, CSS and JavaScript coding, digital drawing on art software programs such as Photoshop and Krita, etc.

Overall evaluation:

I think the artwork came out better than I anticipated. I really struggled with CORS errors and getting the image to even render on the web page and then from there the fight became how to pixelate the image and include a piece of information that would be significant to that specific picture, and the integration of the team’s season record was an effective concept.

I think I could have implemented it better by possibly placing text alongside the artwork so it would be easier to understand what is being represented. I also possibly could’ve reduced the size of the ‘73-9’ data text to refine the details of the image so that the main subject, Stephen Curry, is depicted better.

I also failed to get the pixel/size slider working. I think that would have made interaction with the artwork more interactive and made the entire viewing experience more memorable.
