Blog Post 2 - Critical Reflection – Your proposed UI and UX:

Upon reading Knaflic’s Storytelling with Data my understanding of UI and UX, and their function in effective web development, has been enriched. I now understand that through the use of preattentive attributes, I will be able to make use of the visual hierarchy of information to focus users’ attention and communicate information clearly and concisely. Knaflic discusses the use of visual elements such as hue, line length, spatial position, size, and curvature in order to assist audiences/users in processing information presented to them. With regards to web design and Interactive Media, this can be applied through the means of using elements that rank higher on the visual hierarchy.

The combination of UI/UX principles and preattentive attributes will aid in enhancing important modern web development principles such as aesthetics, accessibility, and user engagement. In my design process emphasis has been placed on the use of preattentive attributes such as coloration, size, and outlining (enclosure) to improve overall user experience. I aim to combine complimentary preattentive attributes in my blog design for instance to incorporate the enclosure of trending topics (outlining) in red boxes/shadows as illustrated below..

In my understanding of Data Driven Storytelling (2018) I have observed that data communication or in this case, information (blog posts), in web development is integral to the ease of access and navigation of the website. The visual-spatial representations of information i.e the use of words and symbolism ensure the communication of information and data presented on the website. Therefore, I aim to direct my attention towards improving my website through this principle essentially by using text (not merely as text but as a means of annotation), numbers, and symbols/shapes. I think the addition of this principle in conjunction with the preattentive attributes will allow for effective information digestion.

The use of the annotation to label and augment the visuals represented through shapes/symbols and, the colorization of the annotations and/or shapes will allow users to consume and understand the information better.

An example is presented below, whereby the preattentive attributes of size and spatial separation have been implemented along with the annotations of “OLD” blog posts to “NEW” in bold capitalized lettering, and the shapes/symbolism of the arrows indicating direction thus allowing users to immediately understand how to navigate the page.

In my opinion the previous example illustrates an improved understanding of a combination of all the elements and principles presented and discussed in the readings and lectures however lacks more of the UI/UX principles of aesthetics and user engagement. I believe the addition of colorization and possibly use of different annotations will aid in communicating information in a way that is more visually stimulating.

This can be implemented by dulling out the blog posts behind the main card and providing visual indication of the number of blog posts either through a scroll bar or card counter. As represented below

I think that the integration of these principles along with other elements of web design that I have learned throughout the semester will greatly improve my website and overall web design skills. I think also having personalization mechanics such as the ability to adjust theme color, highlight trending posts/topics, have a favorite section, and personalized blog post suggestions will allow for greater user engagement and user traffic to the website because not only is the website user friendly the user also enjoys the experience the get out of being on the website. As illustrated below

I will make use of a UI/UX design that is like the ones presented above, I will aim to integrate Source 2D and Source 1A (reminiscent of my previous website design). I will possibly have them on the homepage with Source 2D at the top of the landing page.

I have learned to understand the concepts we have engaged with such as how to tell a story with data, how to declutter a website, and the principles that shape modern web development/web design processes. Previously I believed that aesthetics was the core principle surrounding UI/UX principles and even Knaflic’s elements of information communication but through further interrogation of the readings I have learned that there is a balance between UI/UX and preattentive principles.

Furthermore, each concept though having its own views stems from the pursuit of accessible, easily communicable and understandable information. I am now a better equipped web designer and aim to put my newly acquired skills to use in the continued development of my blog website.

REFERENCE LIST:

Knaflic, C. N., 2015. Storytelling with Data. s.l.:Wiley. Nathalie Henry Riche, C. H. N. D. S. C., 2018. Data-Driven Storytelling. 1st ed. New York: Routledge.
