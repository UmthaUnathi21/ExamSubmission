Blog Post 3 - Close Reading - Overview of Data Visualisation:

Dimara and Perin define interaction for data visualization as external entities and internal actions.

Regarding external entities;

This type of interaction is described as a “dialogue between the user and the visualization system” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) thus it is said that for interaction to occur input from the user (human entity) is necessary for the visualization system (CPU entity) to communicate an output. This varies from internal actions where the plane of interaction takes place within the user themselves. This process is described as a cognitive process in which matters of emotions, and mental health are factors. I think the authors manage to effectively communicate the necessity of the external and internal nature of interactions.

Furthermore, it is my understanding that for interactions to take place (from Uber ride requests and fitness trackers to sports stats and speedometers) there first must be input from the user i.e., Data visualization systems are unable to produce a change without human intervention.

With the concept of data visualization interaction needing to occur between two parties (human and computer systems) in mind interactions with data visualization systems are founded on concepts such as; granularity, transmission, control, tool use, optimal behavior, embodiment, experience, and interplay. Etc. I believe the concepts that relate well with each other but are also individual systems that impact interactions with data visualizations are; dialogue, optimal behavior, experience, and embodiment.

Through dialogue with data visualization systems, users can “formulate a goal, then execute the actions needed to move that goal forward, perceive the output system state and relate that to their goal” (Dimara and Perin, 2020).

I believe that the dialogue that takes place between the user and the system is communication based on the user's input thus the output system/data visualization system informs the user of their desired request this is seen in search engines such as Google, Yahoo, and Bing! As well as recent technological advancements such as AI software i.e., ChatGPT all of which require input from the user, followed by the output of information for the user related to the specific topic/field they enquired about, then preceded by the user's cognitive process of relating the given information (data) to their objective.

Optimal behavior is related to performance and the communication of information through data visualizations.

Dimara and Perin define optimal behavior as “information foraging...adaptation of motor, perceptual and cognitive processes” (Dimara and Perin, 2020). This idea of “information foraging” is necessary, especially regarding effective information communication in websites and games. It is essential as a game developer and web designer to create systems that will visually promote the communication of data by encouraging exploration and engagement.

If players and users are more interested in figuring out how everything works and where the rest of the information is, then behavioral optimization is achieved. Players and users will spend hours on your game/web system as a result because “utility does not equal efficiency. Sometimes wasting time is considered optimal” and proves favorable to visually communicating data.

The concept of embodiment is an integral concept of discussion.

As it pertains to the modern trends in data visualization. The psychology behind it is users should experience “technology is an extension of them” (Dimara and Perin, 2020). The integration of modern technologies specifically mobile phones into our everyday lives allows users to always have access to data visualization software and systems from the pressing of the power button to switch on your phone screen to check the time, instead of looking at an analog watch to scrolling through social media tapping the screen to like, comment and socially interact with other users on social media.

To a large degree technology has become an extension of ourselves, I personally feel a fundamental void/absence when I have not checked my phone after 15-30 minutes. Essentially our technology has allowed for the infiltration of data visualization interaction daily furthermore it has afforded data visualization systems the ability to dictate our behaviors.

The level to which data visualization has seeped into modern culture/society is evident in that data visualization with regards to the number of or length of interactions themselves have become a profitable market. Corporate giants clammer for the attention of users, who have now become consumers as the length, number and type of interactions, with posts on social media, is measured where the companies with the highest rate of interactions gain larger profits.

The concept of experience i.e., User Experience is essential to data visualization systems and is described as a “key factor in how interaction unfolds” (Dimara and Perin, 2020).

By leveraging the idea of “interplay between a person and a data interface” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) one can capture the attention of users and large volumes of audiences thus using the data system to create ‘internal actions’ within the person i.e., excitement and anticipation. I understand the concept of experience in relation to interaction as a process of internal engagement within the user, which occurs because of the data visualization system communicating through themes such as aesthetics and stimulation.

This is evident in advertisements on YouTube whereby companies create short eye-catching (aesthetics) and emotionally striking pieces with music to capture the attention of audiences to purchase their products. I believe that data visualization interaction with regards to user experience is a very effective technique/concept.

I believe that the above-mentioned concepts are all related with regard to the idea that;

The idea that data visualization interactions need to take place between two parties (human and computer system) because dialogue cannot take place in the absence of a second party much less a party that is incapable of providing an input thus no output will be provided, this leads to the concept of optimal behavior because in order for users to “forage” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) and explore successfully on websites and in games the user/player has to formulate a goal, then communicate what they want to achieve through dialogue with the visualization system.

The system needs to perform the actions needed to get the user to their desired destination, the concepts of embodiment and experience are integral for the perception of the information visually displayed by the output system and being able to relate that to the user’s internal cognitive process and motivations. The relationship between the perception of the information and its relation to the user, personally is the most important part of interaction and data visualization because the more intrapersonal that data is perceived the more time the user/player will spend locating and dissecting the data on your website or game.

REFERENCE LIST:

Dimara, E. and Perin, C. (2020) ‘What is interaction for data visualization?’, IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics, 26(1), pp. 119–129. doi:10.1109/tvcg.2019.2934283.
