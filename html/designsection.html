Overview of Design Section:

Data visualization using wireframes is the most important part of designing and creating a website. it is the foundation of all that will be done and performed on the website.

I've had to redesign my website layout and design due to clashes in information communication and overall sight functionability

To be honest I have struggled with applying the techniques taught in lectures. However, I have developed a sound design framwork.

For example, the homepage and the addition of the API search box. I hade to re-align the text as it was not matching with the textbox itself. Even now you can still see that the post cards are too close to the search button

I also faced struggles with the actual rendering of the image as I wanted it to be centered and aligned with the heading and search box. I had to adjust my code to render the entire image to give the illusion of being aligned.

Furthermore, once that problem was solved was the issue of the title of the picture being off center. Which I then had to readjust manually till aesthtically pleasing.

My initial colour palette consisted of black, white and grey tones of different variations. In this I was attempting to achieve an elegant, modern and classy look. However, I received feedback that whilst the aesthetic was good it was dull and took away from the site as a whole.

I then implemented a starry backdrop through js code to provide some level of depth and further entrench the space/earthy/geographical theme of the website. The attempt was valiant but left a lot of information impossible to read.

Ultimately I reverted to a more traditional style but opted to incorporate some space-esque and NASA-like colours such as red and blue with hints of faint grey-blue. I believe this worked well because there is contrast in the sight and users are able to understand what buttons to use and where to go based off the right amount of colour variation.
